Ora / Local-first fitness app
Athlete-owned data

Private logs, portable reports, opt-in sharing.

Ora treats workout, nutrition, progress, and coaching data as sensitive context. Cloud-backed web access should be useful and explicit.

What Ora supports

  • Training and nutrition logs should remain useful without permanent network dependency.
  • Health-related signals and coach context should be handled conservatively.
  • Users should understand which surfaces are local, account-backed, or shared.
  • Report exports should preserve caveats and avoid leaking private source material.

Why it matters

The coach/report direction works only if sharing is athlete-granted, scoped, revocable, and understandable.

Local-first fitness app FAQ

How does this connect to reports?

Ora keeps captured data, source quality, and missing context together so review packets can show both signal and uncertainty.

Does Ora replace coaching judgment?

No. Ora organizes evidence and feedback boundaries. Athletes and coaches still decide what to do with the review.

Ora is developed by Raul Valle for lifters who want training, nutrition, progress reports, and coach review in one accountable system.
