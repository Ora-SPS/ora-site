How many hard sets per muscle per week for hypertrophy?

The honest answer is a range, not a magic number. Here is what the research broadly suggests, what a “hard set” actually means, and how to find the volume that works for you.

The short answer

For most lifters, a reasonable starting point is roughly 10–15 hard sets per muscle per week, then adjust up or down based on how you recover and whether you’re actually progressing. Meaningful growth can happen with fewer; many trained lifters do well somewhere in the region of 10–20 sets; and past a personal ceiling, extra sets mostly add fatigue rather than muscle.

What counts as a “hard set”

This is where most volume advice falls apart. A hard set is a working set taken close to failure — about 0–4 reps in reserve — through a full range of motion, for the muscle you’re counting. Warm-ups and sets you stop well short of real effort don’t count. Ten genuinely hard sets are a very different stimulus from ten easy ones.

What the research broadly suggests

Across volume meta-analyses (the work of Schoenfeld and colleagues is a good entry point), a few patterns hold up reasonably well:

  • There’s a dose-response. More weekly hard sets generally means more growth — up to a point.
  • The point of diminishing returns is individual. Returns flatten and recovery cost climbs as volume rises; the exact ceiling varies a lot between people.
  • Low volume still works. Even a handful of hard sets per muscle per week can drive growth, which matters if you’re short on time.
  • Proximity to failure and exercise selection change how much each set “counts.”

Caveat worth stating plainly: study averages are not you. These ranges describe groups; your own response depends on training age, recovery, sleep, nutrition, stress, and exercise choice.

How to find your number

  1. Start in the middle. ~10–15 hard sets per muscle per week.
  2. Hold it for a few weeks. One session isn’t signal — trends are.
  3. Watch recovery and performance. If sets are still getting harder to recover from and reps/loads are stalling or dropping, you may be past your recoverable volume.
  4. Adjust one variable at a time. Add or remove a couple of sets, then re-check against the same signals.

FAQ

How many hard sets per muscle per week should I do for hypertrophy?
There’s no single number. A practical starting point is ~10–15 hard sets per muscle per week; many trained lifters land in the ~10–20 region, with diminishing returns and rising recovery cost beyond that.

What counts as a “hard set”?
A working set taken close to failure (~0–4 reps in reserve), full range of motion, for the target muscle. Warm-ups and easy sets don’t count.

Is more volume always better?
No. More tends to help up to a point, then extra sets add fatigue without extra growth.
