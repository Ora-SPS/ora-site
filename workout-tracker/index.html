Ora / Workout tracker
Training evidence

Workout tracking built for report quality.

Ora captures sets, reps, load, effort, substitutions, and plan adherence so a training report can explain what actually happened.

What Ora supports

  • Completed sets, working volume, duration, exercise notes, RPE, and RIR.
  • Plan-vs-actual status for completed, modified, skipped, and substituted work.
  • Recent history and observed progression signals labeled as log evidence, not verified guarantees.
  • Flags for pain, fatigue, and missing context before coaching recommendations are made.

Why it matters

A workout tracker should make the next training decision easier without pretending one session proves a trend.

Workout tracker FAQ

How does this connect to reports?

Ora keeps captured data, source quality, and missing context together so review packets can show both signal and uncertainty.

Does Ora replace coaching judgment?

No. Ora organizes evidence and feedback boundaries. Athletes and coaches still decide what to do with the review.

Ora is built for lifters who want training, nutrition, progress reports, and coach review in one accountable system.
