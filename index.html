How it works

One system, from today to your next block.

Open it before you lift, log without friction, adjust the day before the day adjusts you, then review what changed.

  1. 01 / Today

    Know what matters before you train.

    Open Ora and see today’s planned session, your readiness, and one suggested adjustment — before you touch a barbell.

  2. 02 / Train

    Log sets without fighting the app.

    Fast set entry, rest, next-set targets, and quick substitutions — logging keeps up with the session instead of slowing it down.

  3. 03 / Recalculate

    Adjust the day before the day adjusts you.

    Ask Ora to adjust today’s work. It drafts a change, shows one reason and a confidence level, and waits for you to apply or ignore it.

FAQ

Questions before joining.

Who is Ora for?

Serious lifters — powerlifters, bodybuilders, and powerbuilders who care about strength, muscle, and real progress. It stays beginner-friendly, but it’s built around intentional training.

Does the coach actually change my plan?

Ora drafts a change to today’s session with one reason and a confidence level. Nothing changes until you apply it — you stay in control.

Does the AI pretend to be certain?

No. Recommendations carry a confidence level and estimates are labeled. Missing context stays visible instead of being hidden.

Can Ora analyze my form from video?

No. Camera workflows start with barcode, label, and progress-photo capture — not form diagnosis. We won’t claim what the product can’t do.

How much will it cost?

There’s a Free tier to start, Ora Plus at a proposed $4.99/mo (or $39.99/yr — save 33%), and Ora Pro at a tentative $14.99/mo (advanced AI and richer reports) available after launch. Billing is not live during the private beta and prices may change.
